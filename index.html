Today's Focus, made whole.

Two briefings a day on Ryan's Notion workspace. The morning brief shows what to focus on this week — only things due in the next 7 days — plus rolled-up flags for any client × service whose milestone chain has gaps in Asana. The afternoon brief shows what slipped, what's new, and what was cleared.

  • What Ryan does each day: opens the morning brief at 08:00 · works the focus list · glances at the chain-gap flags · checks the afternoon brief at 16:00 to see what slipped.
  • How it decides what to surface: every open subtask in Asana is scored against the milestone chain it lives inside. Each service template has thresholds (e.g. monthly report turns yellow at T-7, orange at T-3, red at T-1) and the system computes days-until-due, chain progress, Claude-reasoned effort, and client sensitivity. Output is one of three colours within this week's window: 🔴 act today · 🟠 this week · 🟢 background. Items further than 7 days out aren't shown.
  • How chain gaps surface: the morning run cross-checks each client × service against the milestone chain in its Notion Service Template. One rolled-up flag per (client × service) with gaps — e.g. "EMPAC · Quarterly BAS — has milestones coming up that aren't in Asana yet". No 10-page list of every missing subtask. The system does not write to Asana in this build — flags are informational only.
  • Scope: Ryan's workspace, this-week focus only. The 21-day "on the radar" wrap-up, manual one-click auto-create per flag, and full auto-create are explicit next-step iterations.

01 · Finding

Today's Focus surfaces what's there. Nothing checks what's missing.

  • Ryan's work lives in subtask names, not assignee fields. The briefing reads parent + subtask structure, not just top-level tasks.
  • Asana custom-field metadata is essentially empty across all 10 client projects. Industry, AM, state, complexity, service mix — none of it lives in Asana.
0of10
Asana client projects with structured client metadata. All of it lives in the Notion Client Register and Services databases. Asana is the execution surface; Notion is the source of truth.
  • This means: whether the right tasks even exist in Asana cannot be verified from inside Asana. It can only be verified by reading the Notion Service Templates milestone chains and comparing against what's present per client project.
  • No system does this today. Tasks go missing silently. Today's Focus has no way to know — it surfaces a 3–5 priority list from a task graph that may already be incomplete.

And a third finding · chain progress is invisible

  • Even when a service's tasks exist in Asana, Asana has no native concept of "this BAS is 2 of 10 milestones done with 3 days to go." Each milestone is just a task in a list. There's no shape to the chain.
  • That shape is what tells Ryan which service is actually at risk. 2-of-10 due Friday needs his attention now. 9-of-10 due Friday is a 30-minute review on Friday morning.
  • The Notion Service Templates already define the canonical milestone chain for each of the 19 services. This system makes that chain a first-class structure in Asana — and uses chain progress as a top-tier scoring signal in Today's Focus.
  • Implication. Three jobs: (1) materialise every service's milestone chain in Asana with computed dates and role-derived assignees, (2) make chain progress visible and quantifiable, (3) surface the result in Today's Focus with reasoning Ryan can trust.

Why two distinct briefings instead of "the same thing twice"?

  • The questions are different. Morning: "What should I work on this week?" Afternoon: "Where am I now versus where I planned to be?"
  • Chain-gap flags only run in the morning. Surfacing them twice adds noise — by 4pm Ryan has already read the morning brief.
  • The afternoon brief is a slip/clear diff against the morning snapshot. It can't compute that until the morning brief has been written.
03 · Urgency

Milestone chains and how a task becomes red, orange, or green.

What a milestone chain is

Every service Nine Advisory delivers — Quarterly BAS, Monthly Bookkeeping, Annual Statements, Weekly Payroll, etc. — has a fixed sequence of milestones that have to happen in order. The Quarterly BAS chain, for example:

Quarterly BAS chain (10 milestones)
──────────────────────────────────────
1 · Gather records from client    offset −50d · offshore_lead
2 · Reconcile bank feeds + GST    offset −43d · offshore_lead
3 · Prepare draft BAS    offset −36d · offshore_lead
4 · Senior review (GST + PAYG)    offset −29d · AM
5 · Send to client for sign-off    offset −22d · AM
...
10 · Close out    offset 0 · AM

  • Chains live in Notion Service Templates, one chain per service. Each milestone has seq, name, days_offset, assignee_role.
  • Each (client × service-instance) has an anchor date — the deadline the chain ends at (BAS lodgement date · payroll run · monthly close). The anchor lives on the Services subscription record.
  • Each milestone's computed due date = anchor + days_offset. days_offset is negative because milestones happen before the anchor. So milestone 4 of EMPAC's Q1 BAS, with anchor 28 May and offset −29d, is due 29 April.
  • Each milestone's assignee = role → person, resolved per client (offshore_lead → Sailesh for EMPAC, Sriram for Hudson; AM → Ryan; etc.).
  • Open subtasks in Asana are matched to their position in this chain. That match — "this is milestone 4 of 10 in EMPAC's Q1 BAS" — is the unit the urgency model scores.

How days until due is measured · the T-N notation

T-N just means "N days until this milestone's computed due date." Every morning, the system computes T-N freshly per subtask:

  • T-7 = due in 7 days · T-3 = due in 3 days · T-1 = due tomorrow · T-0 = due today · T+2 = 2 days overdue.
  • Computed from the milestone's due date — which itself comes from service_template.days_offset + service_instance.anchor_date, not from a free-text Asana date. If Asana's date drifts from the template-computed date, the template wins.
  • Re-computed every run. T-3 this morning becomes T-2 tomorrow morning; the colour can change accordingly.

How a task gets a colour

Per service, the Notion Service Template defines three thresholds — red, orange, yellow — measured in days before due. The colour is then modified by chain progress, effort, and sensitivity. Below is the threshold table per service type:

Service template 🔴 red at 🟠 orange at 🟡 yellow at (future iteration)
Weekly payrollT-0 · due todayT-1T-3
Monthly bookkeepingT-1T-3T-7
Monthly reportT-1T-3T-7
Quarterly BAST-2T-7T-14
Annual statementsT-7T-14T-21

Thresholds are editable in Notion (Service Templates). Ryan tunes them as the system runs. The yellow column is shown for context — yellow is the 21-day on-the-radar wrap-up, which is the next iteration after this build. This build only surfaces red and orange (T-0 to T-7).

What modifies the colour

A subtask first lands in a colour based on its T-N versus the service threshold. Then three modifiers can push it up:

  • Chain progress. If the chain is behind pace — e.g. only 1 of 10 milestones done with 7 days to go — every remaining subtask in that chain bumps up one level. Why: the calendar says T-7 (yellow on a BAS), but at this rate the chain won't actually land. So those subtasks read as orange.
  • Effort estimate. Claude estimates hours-remaining per subtask (see "How Claude estimates effort" below). If est_hours_remaining / days_until_due exceeds Ryan's working capacity, the subtask bumps up one level. Why: a 30-min task at T-3 is fine; a 6h task at T-1 is critical even though both are inside the orange band.
  • Client sensitivity. Clients flagged sensitive in the Client Register bump up one level. One-way — never bumps down.

Worked example · two BAS subtasks, both due in 7 days

Same service template, same T-7. They get different colours because chain progress and effort differ:

  • EMPAC · BAS · "Reconcile bank feed" · T-7 · 1 of 10 chain milestones done · Claude estimates 3h work. → BAS template puts T-7 at orange baseline. Chain progress is far behind, modifier escalates → 🔴 red. Surfaces in Focus this week as critical.
  • Hudson · BAS · "Final review" · T-7 · 8 of 10 done · Claude estimates 30 min. → BAS template puts T-7 at orange baseline, but chain is on pace and effort is small, so no escalation. → 🟠 orange. Surfaces in Focus this week, lower priority.

How Claude estimates effort

  • One Claude Haiku call per new or changed subtask, batched. Inputs: subtask name, parent service template, prior similar-subtask estimates.
  • Outputs cached in the Effort Estimates Notion DB (keyed by Asana task GID): Difficulty band (S / M / L / XL) and estimated hours. Read-only on Asana — nothing is written to Asana custom fields in this build.
  • Re-estimated only when the subtask changes. Steady-state cost is near zero — most tasks keep the same estimate week to week.
04 · Missing-task detection

How the morning brief flags chain gaps · rolled up, not exhaustive.

For every active client × service, Notion's Service Template defines the canonical milestone chain. The morning brief checks whether each chain is fully reflected in Asana. If anything's missing, it surfaces one rolled-up flag per (client × service) — not one row per missing milestone. The system does not write to Asana in this build. Flags are informational; Ryan decides whether to create tasks himself.

Rolled up · what the worker sees

  • One flag row per (client × service-instance) with at least one missing subtask. Not one row per missing milestone — Ryan never gets a 10-page list.
  • The row reads as a heads-up: "EMPAC · Quarterly BAS Q1 — milestones in this chain aren't in Asana yet", with a one-line summary like "3 of 10 milestones missing · earliest is due in 6 days."
  • Tap to expand if he wants the chain detail (full milestone list with computed dates and roles). Most of the time he won't need to — the rolled-up line tells him enough to decide whether to act.
  • If a service-instance's chain isn't in Asana at all (no parent task), the row simply says "full chain not in Asana yet."
  • No tick boxes. No Asana writes. If Ryan wants tasks created, he does that himself in Asana; the flag disappears from the next brief once the system sees them.

How the system detects gaps · step by step

  1. Read the chain from Notion. For each (client × service) the client subscribes to, read the service's milestone chain — an ordered list of {seq, name, days_offset, assignee_role}.
  2. Compute the target chain. For each milestone, compute its due date (anchor_date + days_offset) and resolve role → person via the Client Register.
  3. Read the actual. Pull the parent task + subtasks from the matching Asana project for this service-instance.
  4. Match target to actual. Fuzzy name match (similarity ≥ 0.85; matches below 0.92 noted as low-confidence).
  5. Compute the diff per service-instance. Count missing milestones. Find the earliest missing one (lowest computed due date).
  6. Surface as one rolled-up flag. If missing_count ≥ 1, emit one flag row for the service-instance. Header summarises count and earliest due date; expand reveals the full list.

Two guarantees:

  • Read-only on Asana. The system never writes anything to Asana in this build. No new tasks, no edits, no field updates. Manual one-click auto-create per flag and full auto-create are explicit next-step iterations.
  • Legacy work is never destroyed. When auto-create eventually ships, partial-chain handling will preserve existing subtasks — only missing ones would be added.

Error handling

Failure Outcome
Asana API down Run aborts cleanly. The block shows ⚠️ Asana unavailable, retry at HH:30. No data lost — the system is read-only on Asana.
Notion API down Atomic writes fail-fast. No partial state. Next run resumes.
Claude down The brief renders the deterministic state-bucketed list without LLM reasoning lines. Header notes "↳ reasoning unavailable." Chain-flag detection unaffected.
Mid-run crash Idempotent — next run recomputes from current state. Read-only on Asana means no risk of partial writes.
Matcher false positive (low-confidence fuzzy match) Flagged in the brief header as a warning. Audited weekly; fuzzy threshold tightened from flags.
Matcher false negative (a chain-flag that already exists) Worst case: an extra informational row in the brief. No data harm. Ryan flags it; threshold tightened from there.

Auditability

  • Run Log — one row per brief run. Source counts, surfaced count, chain-flag rows produced, runtime, errors.
  • Decision Log — one row per scored candidate. Computed state, threshold that fired, window applied, chain progress + effort inputs, LLM reasoning. Filterable by client × date × surfaced/dropped.
  • All Ryan-visible. 90-day rolling retention.
