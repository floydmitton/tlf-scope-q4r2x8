Today's Focus, made whole.

Two briefings a day on Ryan's Notion workspace. The morning brief shows what to focus on today, plus any tasks that should be in Asana but aren't — Ryan ticks one box and they get created. The afternoon brief shows what slipped, what was cleared, and what's now on the radar for tomorrow.

  • What Ryan does each day: opens the morning brief at 08:00 · works the list · ticks any "Auto-create" boxes for missing tasks · checks the afternoon brief at 16:00 to see what slipped.
  • How it decides what to surface: every open subtask in Asana is scored against five inputs — calendar, service-specific thresholds, milestone chain progress, Claude-reasoned effort estimate, and client sensitivity. Output is a colour: 🔴 act today · 🟠 this week · 🟡 on the radar · 🟢 background.
  • How it spots missing tasks: the morning run cross-checks each client × service against the milestone chain in its Notion Service Template. Anything in the chain but not in Asana shows up in the brief with an Auto-create button. Nothing is created without Ryan ticking the box.
  • Scope: Ryan's workspace this build. Manoli + meeting-derived commitments are later iterations.
Ships week 3 ~60 hours engineering Two briefings · auto-create gated
01 · Finding

Today's Focus surfaces what's there. Nothing checks what's missing.

  • Ryan's work lives in subtask names, not assignee fields. The briefing reads parent + subtask structure, not just top-level tasks.
  • Asana custom-field metadata is essentially empty across all 10 client projects. Industry, AM, state, complexity, service mix — none of it lives in Asana.
0of10
Asana client projects with structured client metadata. All of it lives in the Notion Client Register and Services databases. Asana is the execution surface; Notion is the source of truth.
  • This means: whether the right tasks even exist in Asana cannot be verified from inside Asana. It can only be verified by reading the Notion Service Templates milestone chains and comparing against what's present per client project.
  • No system does this today. Tasks go missing silently. Today's Focus has no way to know — it surfaces a 3–5 priority list from a task graph that may already be incomplete.

And a third finding · chain progress is invisible

  • Even when a service's tasks exist in Asana, Asana has no native concept of "this BAS is 2 of 10 milestones done with 3 days to go." Each milestone is just a task in a list. There's no shape to the chain.
  • That shape is what tells Ryan which service is actually at risk. 2-of-10 due Friday needs his attention now. 9-of-10 due Friday is a 30-minute review on Friday morning.
  • The Notion Service Templates already define the canonical milestone chain for each of the 19 services. This system makes that chain a first-class structure in Asana — and uses chain progress as a top-tier scoring signal in Today's Focus.
  • Implication. Three jobs: (1) materialise every service's milestone chain in Asana with computed dates and role-derived assignees, (2) make chain progress visible and quantifiable, (3) surface the result in Today's Focus with reasoning Ryan can trust.
02 · Ryan's day

Two briefings. One workspace.

Both briefings render to the "🎯 Today's Focus" block on Ryan's existing Notion AM Workspace page. The 08:00 brief is the planning view. The 16:00 brief is the close-out view. Each replaces the previous one in the same spot.

The morning brief · 08:00

What Ryan sees. Three sections, in order:

  1. 🎯 Focus today — the 3–5 things that matter most right now. Each item shows the service, what's blocking it, chain progress (e.g. "2 of 10 done"), and an estimated hours-remaining figure.
  2. 🟡 On the radar — up to 5 items he should be aware of but doesn't need to act on. Early visibility into work that will become urgent in the next few days.
  3. 🔧 Missing from Asana — any client × service whose milestone chain has gaps. Each row shows which subtasks are missing and an "Auto-create" tick box. Ticking the box creates exactly those subtasks in Asana within ~5 seconds, with computed dates and role-assigned people. Nothing is created without Ryan's tick.

What Ryan does. Reads the focus list. Works the items. Ticks any Auto-create boxes for missing chain subtasks he wants created. That's it.

The afternoon brief · 16:00

What Ryan sees. A different shape — designed for end-of-day review, not planning:

  1. 🔴 Slipped from this morning — items that were on the morning focus list and are still open. Each shows why it didn't move.
  2. ⚠️ New today, still open — anything that escalated to red or orange during the day (a deadline crossed a threshold, a chain fell behind, a sensitive client emailed). Catches things that didn't exist this morning.
  3. ✅ Cleared today — count of what was completed since 08:00, with a one-line summary.

What Ryan does. Reviews slipped + new items. Decides what to roll into tomorrow. The afternoon brief does not show missing-task detection — that's a once-a-day morning concern.

Why two distinct briefings instead of "the same thing twice"?

  • The questions are different. Morning: "What should I work on?" Afternoon: "Where am I now versus where I planned to be?"
  • The morning brief includes everything that should be on Ryan's plate today — including missing-task detection, which is the only daily moment where he needs to gate creates. Surfacing that twice a day adds noise.
  • The afternoon brief is a slip/clear diff against the morning snapshot. It can't compute that until the morning brief has been written.
03 · Urgency

How a task gets a colour.

Every open subtask in Asana is scored once per briefing run. The output is one of four colours: 🔴 act today · 🟠 this week · 🟡 on the radar · 🟢 background. Five inputs feed the decision — four are deterministic, one is reasoned by Claude.

Input What it tells the system Source
1. Days until due How much calendar runway is left. Negative = overdue. Asana
2. Service threshold What "urgent" means for this kind of work. A monthly report turns orange at 3 days out; a BAS turns orange at 7 days out; a weekly payroll turns orange at 1 day out. Each service has its own rhythm, defined once in the Notion Service Template. Notion · Service Template
3. Chain progress Whether the milestone chain will actually finish in time at the current pace. 1 of 10 done at T-7 = chain won't land. 9 of 10 done at T-3 = it's fine. Asana · parent + subtasks
4. Effort estimate Reasoned by Claude. From the subtask name, service template description, and any task history, Claude returns a difficulty band (S / M / L / XL) and estimated hours. Cached on the Asana task. A 30-minute task at T-3 reads differently from a 6-hour task at T-3. Claude · cached on the task
5. Sensitivity flag If the client is flagged sensitive in the Client Register, the state is bumped up one level. One-way; never bumps down. Notion · Client Register

The four colours

Colour What it means Example
🔴 Critical Act today or it slips. Past the service's red threshold, overdue, or chain + effort say it can't finish at current pace. Monthly bookkeeping at T-1 · BAS at T-2 · anything overdue · 6h of work left at T-1
🟠 This week Past the orange threshold. Should land in the next few days. Needs planning. Monthly report at T-3 · BAS at T-7 · weekly payroll at T-1
🟡 On the radar Past the yellow threshold. Early visibility — Ryan should know it exists, not act yet. Monthly report at T-7 · BAS at T-14 · annual statements at T-30
🟢 Background Before the yellow threshold. Not surfaced. Anything earlier than its service's yellow point

Worked example · two BAS subtasks, both due in 7 days

Same service, same calendar runway, different colours — because chain progress and effort differ:

  • EMPAC · BAS · "Reconcile bank feed" · T-7 · 1 of 10 chain subtasks done · Claude estimates 3h work. → 🟠 orange. The BAS template would normally make T-7 yellow, but chain progress is too far behind, so it escalates one level.
  • Hudson · BAS · "Final review" · T-7 · 8 of 10 done · Claude estimates 30min. → 🟢 green. T-7 would normally be yellow, but progress + effort show it's safely on track.

How Claude estimates effort

  • One Claude (Haiku) call per new or changed subtask. Inputs: subtask name, parent service, service template description, any task description or comments, prior similar-subtask estimates.
  • Outputs are written to two custom fields on the Asana task: Difficulty (S / M / L / XL) and Est. Hours (number). Both are visible to Ryan and editable — if he overrides them, the system never overwrites his value.
  • Re-estimated only when the task itself changes. Steady-state cost is near zero — most tasks keep the same estimate week to week.
04 · Missing-task detection

How the morning brief spots gaps in Asana.

For every active client × service, Notion's Service Template defines the canonical milestone chain — an ordered list of subtasks with their offsets and assignee roles. The morning brief checks whether each chain is fully reflected in Asana. If anything's missing, it surfaces the gap with an Auto-create tick.

What the worker sees

  • One row per (client × service) with at least one missing subtask.
  • Each row lists the missing subtasks with their computed due dates and role-assigned people, e.g. "EMPAC · Q1 BAS — 3 missing: Reconcile bank feed (Sailesh, due 30 Apr), Senior review (Ryan, due 4 May), Lodge (Ryan, due 7 May)."
  • One Auto-create tick per row creates all the missing subtasks for that service-instance in Asana — never per-subtask, always per-service-instance, so Ryan doesn't tick ten boxes for one BAS.
  • Tick → tasks created within ~5 seconds → row updates to "✓ Created — view in Asana".

How the system detects gaps · step by step

  1. Read the chain from Notion. For each (client × service) the client subscribes to, read the service's milestone chain — an ordered list of {seq, name, days_offset, assignee_role}.
  2. Compute the target. For each milestone, compute its due date (anchor_date + days_offset) and resolve role → person (e.g. offshore_lead → Sailesh) using the Client Register.
  3. Read the actual. Pull the parent task + subtasks from the matching Asana project for this service-instance.
  4. Match target to actual. Subtasks created by this system carry a hidden Milestone Template ID — those match deterministically. Legacy subtasks fall back to fuzzy name match (similarity ≥ 0.85; matches below 0.92 flagged as low-confidence).
  5. Compute the diff. Anything in the target chain with no match in Asana is a gap. Existing subtasks are never touched.
  6. Surface in the morning brief. One row per (client × service) with gaps. Auto-create tick creates only the missing subtasks under the existing parent.

Two guarantees:

  • Nothing is created without Ryan's tick. The system is detect-and-suggest. It will never auto-write to Asana on its own.
  • Legacy work is never destroyed. If 7 of 10 milestones already exist in Asana from earlier work, only the 3 missing ones are created. Pre-write similarity check guards against duplicates.

08 · Walkthrough

One morning brief, end to end.

Runtime is Cowork (Anthropic's agentic runtime) — Floyd's existing stack. No new infrastructure. The walkthrough below is one morning brief on Tuesday 28 April with synthetic data; total elapsed ~3 minutes.

Integration model

System MCP connector Auth What this system does with it
Asana asana-mcp OAuth on Floyd's account Reads tasks + subtasks (with Milestone Template ID, Difficulty, Est. Hours custom fields). Writes new subtasks when Ryan ticks Auto-create. Writes effort estimates back to custom fields.
Notion notion-mcp Workspace integration token Reads Service Templates (chains + thresholds), Client Register, Services, Interaction Log, Email Triage, Briefing State. Writes the "🎯 Today's Focus" marker block on Ryan's AM Workspace page (atomic). Writes Briefing State, Auto-create Log, Run Log, Decision Log.

Notion write pattern

  • One marker block — "🎯 Today's Focus" on Ryan's existing AM Workspace page — rewritten atomically at 08:00 and 16:00 via PATCH /v1/blocks/{marker_id}/children. Replaced as a unit, never partially rendered.
  • Auto-create reads tick state from the rendered block (Notion's to_do block has a checked property), so the tick lives directly on the brief — no separate DB to flip between.
  • Failure mode: previous render persists until next successful run; block header shows "⚠️ Last run failed at HH:MM."

Error handling

Failure Outcome
Asana API down Run aborts cleanly. The block shows ⚠️ Asana unavailable, retry at HH:30. Auto-create ticks remain pending until next successful run.
Notion API down Atomic writes fail-fast. No partial state. Next run resumes.
Claude down The brief renders the deterministic state-bucketed list without LLM reasoning lines. Header notes "↳ reasoning unavailable." Auto-create + missing-task detection unaffected.
Mid-run crash Idempotent — next run recomputes from current state. Auto-create drain checks Asana for already-existing template-ID matches before writing, so a partial drain that replays won't duplicate.
Matcher false positive (low-confidence fuzzy match) Flagged in the brief header as a warning. Audited weekly; fuzzy threshold tightened from flags.
Auto-create duplicate risk Pre-write similarity check ≥ 0.85 against existing subtasks within the same parent → skip + log.

Auditability

  • Auto-create Log — one row per ticked Auto-create. Records what was created, when, runtime, success.
  • Run Log — one row per brief run. Source counts, surfaced count, missing-task rows produced, runtime, errors.
  • Decision Log — one row per scored candidate. Computed state, threshold that fired, chain progress + effort inputs, LLM reasoning. Filterable by client × date × surfaced/dropped.
  • All Ryan-visible. 90-day rolling retention.
